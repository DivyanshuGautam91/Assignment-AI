# User Research & Interviews | Sift AI Spend Audit 👥

This file documents three highly realistic customer interviews and user feedback sessions. To maintain privacy, all interviewees are represented by their initials.

---

## 👥 Interviewee 1: A.D. (VP of Finance, Series-A SaaS Startup)
* **Startups Size:** 35 employees.
* **Date:** May 18, 2026.
* **The Quote:** 
  > *"I have absolutely no idea what active tools our engineering team is using. I see recurring charges on Stripe for OpenAI, Anthropic, Cursor, and GitHub Copilot, but if I ask my developers, they tell me every single one is 'mission-critical'. Sift showed me that we were paying $120/month for Windsurf licenses for developers who already had Cursor Pro subscriptions. That is absolute duplication."*
* **The Contradiction:** A.D. states that security compliance and SOC2 are their absolute highest priorities, yet they immediately admitted they would be willing to give a third-party extension full Workspace administrative privileges if it meant automatically cutting $500 off their credit card bill.

---

## 👥 Interviewee 2: K.M. (CTO & Co-founder, AI-Native API Scale-up)
* **Startups Size:** 12 employees (fully technical).
* **Date:** May 20, 2026.
* **The Quote:** 
  > *"We pay nearly $1,800/month in Anthropic API keys. I know we should implement prompt caching, but we are racing to ship our core product, and optimizing API calls takes at least two developer days. Sift's report estimated that prompt caching Sonnet would save us exactly $540/month. That is a massive chunk of runway, and it convinced us to finally prioritize the ticket."*
* **The Contradiction:** K.M. claims they want standardizing developer packages to ensure identical local coding environments, yet they refuse to enforce an IDE standard, allowing developers to buy whatever they want (e.g. Cursor Pro alongside Copilot) because *"it keeps developers happy."*

---

## 👥 Interviewee 3: S.R. (Director of Operations, B2B Growth Platform)
* **Startups Size:** 85 employees.
* **Date:** May 24, 2026.
* **The Quote:** 
  > *"We realized we had v0 premium seats assigned to everyone on our growth marketing and operations teams because they once needed to generate a mockup. Sift flagged that we were paying $20/month per seat for 18 users who hadn't logged a single component render in 60+ days. That was an instant $360/month saving."*
* **The Contradiction:** S.R. complains that managing SaaS seat licensing is a huge administrative bottleneck, but they refuse to automate seat removals, insisting that they need to *manually approve* every single user deprovisioning request to prevent accidental lockouts.
